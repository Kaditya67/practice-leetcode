# Contest Strategy and Competitive Programming Guide

Competitions require speed, accuracy, and tactical decision-making. Use this guide to optimize your performance in LeetCode Weekly, CodeChef Starters, and GFG PotD.

---

## During the Contest

### 1. Time Management (The 15-30-45 Rule)
- Easy (Problem 1): Goal should be 10-15 minutes.
- Medium (Problem 2/3): Accuracy-first target is 30-35 minutes each.
- Hard (Problem 4): Attempt after medium stability; do not force hard timing in early phase.

### 2. Dealing with "The Wall"
- If you are stuck for more than 15 minutes on a medium problem without progress, switch to the next problem. Return later with a fresh state.
- If you receive a TLE (Time Limit Exceeded), your complexity is wrong. Do not try to "optimize" a bad algorithm; rethink the approach.
- If you receive a WA (Wrong Answer), think about edge cases (0, 1, negatives, overflow).

---

## Problem Solving Workflow in Contests
1. Read accurately: Many points are lost by misunderstanding a single sentence.
2. Penalties count: Check your code against your edge cases before submitting. One wrong submission adds 5 minutes to your total time.
3. Don't over-engineer: In a contest, prioritize a working $O(N)$ solution over an elegant but harder-to-implement $O(1)$ solution if both pass.

---

## Post-Contest: The Growth Phase (Most Important)

### 1. Upsolving
The real learning happens after the timer stops. You MUST solve the problems you missed during the contest.
- Read official editorials.
- Watch video explanations (Striver, NeetCode, etc.).
- Re-implement the solution from scratch without looking at the code.
- If a missed problem took more than 45 minutes or required hints, add it to `SPACED_REPETITION_LOG.md`.

### 2. Sentiment Analysis
- Was I slow in typing? (Practice speed).
- Was I unable to recognize the pattern? (Re-read Study Guides).
- Was I afraid of the Hard problem? (Practice more Hard tags).

### 3. Weekly Sync Check
- Confirm your weekly lock in `Roadmap.md` is filled:
  - Current Week Focus
  - Allowed Detours
  - Success Gate
- Confirm no contradictions across:
  - `README.md`
  - `Roadmap.md`
  - `MASTER_PLAN.md`
  - `CURRICULUM.md`

---
Professionalism and consistency beat talent in the long run.
